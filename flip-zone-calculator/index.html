The Verdict

Beyond the Money

Financial comparison is only one variable. Other factors that matter:

  • Development: Some players develop faster against NBA competition. Others benefit from being the focal point of a college offense.
  • Injury risk: Every additional college season carries risk with no guaranteed NBA contract as insurance.
  • Draft class strength: A weaker draft class next year could push a prospect higher. A stronger one could push them lower.
  • NIL deal durability: The CSC has rejected 524 deals worth $14.94M. Not all college compensation is guaranteed to survive regulatory scrutiny.
  • Age: NBA teams generally prefer younger prospects. Returning to school means entering the league older, which can affect perceived upside.
